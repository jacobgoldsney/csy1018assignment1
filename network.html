My Pathway


Networks are a vital component of the modern world and our dependence on technology further increases the importance of maintaining networks. As a network engineer it is your responsibility to maintain the services provided by networks, amongst a wide range of other roles. Some examples of roles you can expect to do as a network engineer include:

-The design and implementation of new networks and improving security of the current environment;
- Maximizing network efficiency by monitoring performance, troubleshooting network problems, scheduling upgrades and collaborating with other network engineers on optimising the network;
- Securing networks by creating and enforcing security policies, and defining and controlling access;
- Keeping up-to-date by participating in educational opportunities, reading professional publications and maintaining personal networks;
- Updating network equipment to the latest stable firmware releases;
- Configuration of routing and switching equipment;
- Configuration of firewalls;
- remote troubleshooting;
- capacity management and IP addressing/management - Communicating with project managers, other engineers and service desk engineers on a regular basis;

There are various qualifications that will help in pursuing a career in network engineering, the first of which is a computing related degree, helps securing a job in this field, however it is favourable if it is a networking specific degree. Also, there are various cisco qualifications which are industrially recognised and important to upkeeping your knowledge.

To me, being a network engineer is something that I find interesting because I enjoy solving problems and keeping up-to-date with computer technology. Maintaining and monitoring my personal devices and network is something which I do, and enjoy, anyway so learning how to use more advanced technologies is something that I would consider a hobby.
